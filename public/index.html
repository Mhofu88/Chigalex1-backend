FAQ
Frequently Asked Questions

Admin-added FAQs appear at the top tagged NEW. Core Africa-wide questions below.

Is Pi Network free to join?▾
Yes — joining Pi Network is completely free across Africa. You only need a smartphone and a referral code (use chigalex1). The Chigalex1 membership fee (1π) is an independent community app access fee, not a charge by Pi Network or SocialChain Inc.
Do I need to keep the app open to mine Pi?▾
No. Pi mines in the background. You just need to tap the lightning button once every 24 hours to start a new session. Works on any smartphone — even basic Android phones common across Africa.
My KYC document was rejected. What do I do?▾
Check the rejection reason in the app. Common issues: blurry photo, glare on document, expired ID, name mismatch. Use good lighting, a flat dark surface, and ensure your ID name matches your Pi account name exactly.
What is GCV and why does it matter for African businesses?▾
GCV (Global Consensus Value) is the agreed value of 1 Pi coin. It has not yet been officially decided. African businesses joining the enclosed ecosystem NOW are positioned as first movers. When GCV is established, these businesses already have Pi reserves and customer bases.
Can a business register without the owner completing personal KYC?▾
No. The business owner must first complete personal KYC before submitting a KYB application. Both verifications are required for full business status in the Pi enclosed ecosystem.
What is the Pi enclosed ecosystem economy?▾
An active economy within Pi Network where KYB-verified businesses accept Pi as payment from KYC-verified pioneers. It operates now — before the Open Mainnet.
What is Map of Pi and why should my African business list there?▾
mapofpi.com is the official Pi business directory shown on a live map. Listing is free after KYB approval and gives your business visibility to 50M+ pioneers worldwide.
How do I report a Pi scam?▾
Report via the Pi app's support section, or email support@minepi.com. Pi scams are particularly active in Nigeria, Kenya, South Africa, and Ghana — always verify any Pi-related communication comes from inside the official Pi app.
