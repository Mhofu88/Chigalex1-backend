🔄 What is PiDEX?

Pi Network's Own Built-In Exchange — Live Since March 12, 2026

Think of it like this: A regular market where people buy and sell goods — except instead of tomatoes and bread, people are exchanging Pi tokens for other digital tokens, directly with each other, with no middleman taking a cut.

PiDEX stands for Pi Decentralized Exchange. It lives inside your Pi Wallet and Pi Browser. It uses a system called an Automated Market Maker (AMM) — a smart computer programme that automatically sets fair prices based on supply and demand, just like a market does naturally.

💡

Simple version: PiDEX = a marketplace inside Pi where you can exchange your Pi for other project tokens — safely, without leaving the Pi ecosystem.

📚 Key Concepts — Plain Language
🤝

Peer-to-Peer Trading

You trade directly with other pioneers. No bank, no broker, no exchange company taking large fees. The Pi blockchain handles the transaction automatically and safely.

🏊

Liquidity Pools

Think of a pool of water. Pioneers add their Pi to a shared pool. When someone wants to swap tokens, they draw from this pool. In return, pool contributors earn a small reward — called a liquidity fee.

🤖

Automated Market Maker

No human sets prices on PiDEX. A smart mathematical formula automatically calculates fair prices based on how much of each token is available in the pool. More demand = higher price. More supply = lower price.

🛡️

Token Vetting

Not just ANY token can appear on PiDEX. Pi Network reviews and approves tokens before they're listed — protecting pioneers from scam tokens and worthless projects.

📈

Price Discovery

PiDEX helps establish the REAL value of Pi — based on actual trading activity, not speculation. Every swap on PiDEX is a vote for what Pi is truly worth.

⛽

Gas Fees

A tiny fee paid to process your transaction on the blockchain. On PiDEX, fees are kept very low — making it accessible to African pioneers with small balances.

What is the difference between staking and committing?▾
Staking = reserving your Pi (like raising your hand). Committing = actually sending Pi to the pool (like paying). You MUST do both to earn tokens. Staking alone earns you nothing. Many pioneers in the first test launch made this mistake — do not repeat it!

Your Pi goes into a liquidity pool — NOT into the project team's wallet. This pool pairs your Pi with the new project token, making it immediately tradeable on PiDEX. This design prevents rug pulls and ensures the token has real liquidity from launch day.
What happens to Testnet tokens after Mainnet launches?▾
Testnet tokens (like the test token and SLICE) will NOT transfer to Mainnet. They are only for practice and learning. When Mainnet launches, new real token launches will begin. Your advantage is that you will already know how the system works!

Traditional crypto ICOs often raise money with just a whitepaper — no working product. Pi Launchpad requires projects to have a WORKING APP before launching. Money goes to liquidity pools, not founders. This makes Pi Launchpad far safer for pioneers than typical crypto fundraising.
