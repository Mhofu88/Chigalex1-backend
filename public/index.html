KYC (Know Your Customer) verifies you are a real unique human. Required before using Pi on the open mainnet.

303
304
1
Check Your KYC Invitation
Go to Profile → KYC in the Pi app and check your slot.
KYC is rolled out gradually. Open the Pi app → tap your Profile → tap KYC. If your slot is ready you'll see a "Start KYC" button. Keep your app updated to the latest version.
▾
305
2
Prepare Your ID Document
Have a valid government-issued ID matching your Pi account name.

Accepted: National ID Card · Passport · Driver's License · Residence Permit

Must be: valid (not expired) · clearly readable · same name as Pi account · showing photo + date of birth

SADC countries: Zimbabwe · South Africa · Botswana · Zambia · Malawi · Mozambique · Tanzania · DRC · Namibia · Lesotho · Eswatini · Angola · Madagascar · Mauritius · Comoros · Seychelles

▾
306
3
Liveness Check
Look at camera, blink, or move head as instructed by the app.
Sit in good, even lighting. Face the camera directly. Remove glasses if prompted. Follow all on-screen prompts. This proves you are a real live person and not a photo.
▾
307
4
Scan Your Document
Photograph front (and back) of your ID clearly with no glare.
Place your ID on a flat dark surface. Use bright natural light. Avoid glare, shadows, or fingers over text. Review extracted data on screen and correct any errors before submitting.

Wait for Approval & Unlock Wallet
Instant or up to several days. Then import wallet with your passphrase.

Common rejections: blurry photo · glare · expired ID · name mismatch. Fix and resubmit.

Once approved: Pi Wallet → Import wallet → enter your 24-word passphrase. Your mined Pi transfers to mainnet.

⚠️

Only ever enter your passphrase in the official Pi app — never on external sites.

315

The business owner must be personally KYC-approved before applying for KYB.
Go to the KYC tab and complete all 5 steps as an individual. Keep your KYC approval notification. You cannot proceed with KYB without this.

317
2
Access Pi Developer Portal
Open Pi Browser → type pi://develop.pi, or visit minepi.com/developers.

Option A — Pi Browser (recommended): Download Pi Browser from your app store. Navigate to pi://develop.pi

Option B — Desktop: Visit minepi.com/developers. Log in with your Pi credentials. Choose "Business Registration" / "KYB".

3
Prepare Business Documents
Gather registration certificate, tax number, and address proof.

Required:
📄 Certificate of Incorporation / Business Registration [e.g. ZIMRA, SARS, ZRA]
📄 Tax Registration Number certificate
📄 Proof of address — utility bill / bank statement (max 3 months old)
📄 Director's personal KYC documents
📄 Company bank account details

All in English or officially translated. JPG/PNG/PDF, max 5MB each.

Upload all documents and submit.

How to Register on Map of Pi
344

After KYB approval, listing on mapofpi.com puts your business in front of pioneers searching for places to spend Pi — locally and globally.

345
🌍

Map of Pi is the official Pi business directory on a live map. Pioneers use it to find Pi-accepting businesses near them. Free to list after KYB approval.

346
1
Confirm KYB is Approved
Log into the Pi Developer Portal and confirm your business shows status "Approved". If still pending, complete the KYB steps above first.
347
2
Open Map of Pi in Pi Browser
Open the Pi Browser app. In the address bar type mapofpi.com or search "Map of Pi" in the Pi Browser app store. Tap "Sign in with Pi" to authenticate with your Pi account.
348
3
Create Your Business Listing
Tap "Add My Business". Fill in: Business name · Location (enable location permissions for accurate pin) · Category · Short description · Logo or photo · Operating hours · Contact number / WhatsApp · Website or social media link.
349
4
Set Pi Payment Details
Add your Pi username (wallet address) for receiving payments. Set price display preference (Pi, USD, or local currency). Enable the "Accept Pi Payments" toggle so pioneers know you accept Pi.
350
5
Submit & Go Live
Review all details and tap Submit. Your business pin appears on the map within 24–48 hours after moderation. Share your listing link in local Pi communities to drive pioneers to your business.
351
6
Maintain Your Listing
Return regularly to update hours, add new products, upload fresh photos, respond to reviews, and adjust Pi pricing. Active listings rank higher in Map of Pi search results.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions
361 362
363 364
Is Pi Network free to join?▾
Yes — joining Pi Network is completely free. The Chigalex1 membership fee (1π) is an independent community app access fee, not a charge by Pi Network or SocialChain Inc.
365
Do I need to keep the app open to mine?▾
No. Pi mines in the background once you start a session. You just tap the lightning button once every 24 hours to begin a new session. No heavy battery or data usage.
366
My KYC document was rejected. What do I do?▾
Check the rejection reason in the app. Common issues: blurry photo, glare, expired ID, name mismatch. Fix the issue (use good lighting, flat surface, valid ID matching your Pi account name) and resubmit.
367
Can a business do KYB without the owner completing personal KYC?▾
No. The business owner or authorized representative must first complete personal KYC before submitting a KYB application.
368
When can I spend or withdraw my Pi?▾
Pi can be spent with KYB-verified businesses that accept Pi. The Open Mainnet (for external trading) is progressively rolling out — KYC-verified pioneers access it first.
369
What is Map of Pi and why should my business list there?▾
mapofpi.com is the official Pi business directory on a live map. 50+ million pioneers use it to find places to spend Pi. Listing is free after KYB approval and gives your business global and local visibility.
370
Is Pi Network available in my SADC country?▾
Yes — Pi Network is available globally including all 16 SADC member states: Angola, Botswana, Comoros, DRC, Eswatini, Lesotho, Madagascar, Malawi, Mauritius, Mozambique, Namibia, Seychelles, South Africa, Tanzania, Zambia, and Zimbabwe.
371
How do I report a Pi scam?▾
Report via the Pi app's support section, email support@minepi.com, or contact @PiCoreTeam on Twitter/X. Never send Pi or money to anyone claiming to multiply it or pretending to be Pi staff asking for your passphrase.
